SGEB Symposium

Introduction

During 2025, our team organized the 1st Gaucho Symposium on Biomolecular Engineering, held on November 11th, 12th, and 13th at the Biotechnology Center Auditorium of the Federal University of Rio Grande do Sul, in Porto Alegre.

The symposium was created with the goal of strengthening connections between academia, industry, and students interested in emerging areas of biotechnology. Throughout the three-day event, 18 speakers presented their work and spoke about topics in Molecular Biology, Synthetic Biology, Bioprocesses and Bioinformatics, promoting interdisciplinary dialogue and scientific dissemination. Details on the speakers and the lectures given can be found below.

The SGEB gathered participants from different backgrounds, including undergraduate and graduate students, professors, researchers, and biotechnology companies. Registration was offered in different participation formats in order to increase accessibility and engagement. A total of 42 participants attended the event in person, while 33 joined through the hybrid modality and 24 participated remotely, resulting in 75 participants involved in the in-person and hybrid activities and 99 total registered participants overall.

In addition, the symposium received support from academic institutions and biotechnology companies that contributed both as sponsors and participants in the entrepreneurship session. Institutional support was provided by Programa de Pós Graduação em Biologia Celular e Molecular (PPGBCM). The event was also sponsored by Incubadora Empresarial do Centro de Biotecnologia da UFRGS (IECBiot) and companies including Agronômica, Equipal, Promega, Pró-Análise, ServyLab, Biosumos, Alesco and Exxtend. These organizations contributed to discussions on innovation, entrepreneurship and the industrial applications of biotechnology, while also helping to strengthen the connection between academia and industry.

By organizing this event, our team aimed to create a collaborative environment where participants could exchange experiences, discuss current scientific challenges, and expand their professional and academic networks. The symposium also provided students with the opportunity to interact directly with researchers and industry professionals, encouraging interest in Synthetic Biology and related fields.

DAY 1 (11/11/2025)

Opening Lecture: “iGEM and Synthetic Biology in Brazil: Innovation, Education, and Social Impact”

Speaker: Dr. Diego Bonatto
Professor at Federal University of Rio Grande do Sul, working in the fields of Synthetic Biology, Microbiology, Gene Editing using Yeasts, and Synthetic Biology

This lecture provided a global and national overview of Synthetic Biology, explaining its fundamental concepts and the practice of engineering biological systems. Challenges and advances in the field in Brazil were discussed, highlighting the intersection between research, education, innovation, and social impact. Finally, the lecture explored the role of initiatives such as International Genetically Engineered Machine Foundation in training new scientists and strengthening the strategic position of Latin America within the global scientific landscape.

“Microbial Metataxonomy to Implement Technological Routes for Waste Valorization and Renewable Energy Generation”

Speaker: Dra. Suelen Paesi
Professor at University of Caxias do Sul, working in the field of Microbiology, with emphasis on Applied Microbiology for biogas and methane production.

This lecture explored the importance of anaerobic digestion for waste valorization and renewable energy production, highlighting the role of microbial communities in these bioprocesses. It also discussed how next-generation sequencing technologies can be used to characterize microorganisms and optimize sustainable production chains.

iGEM UFRGS Team Presentation: “iGEM UFRGS Team”

Speaker: Isabelli Seiler
Biotechnology undergraduate student at Federal University of Rio Grande do Sul and research fellow in Synthetic Biology and Molecular Biology at the Computational and Molecular Biology Laboratory (LBCM), under the supervision of Diego Bonatto.

This lecture presented the iGEM UFRGS Team and our project: the development of a Synthetic Biology-based biosensor designed to detect and monitor 2,4-D concentrations directly on leaf surfaces, providing farmers and regulatory agencies with a rapid, simple, and low-cost tool to evaluate contamination by this herbicide.

“Systems Biology and Lignin Biotechnology for the Bioeconomy”

Speaker: Dr. Igor Cesarino
Professor at University of São Paulo, working in the fields of Systems Biology and Plant Biotechnology.

This lecture explored the role of lignin in plant development and its economic importance for the bioeconomy. It highlighted how Systems Biology approaches, including transcriptomics and metabolomics, can be used to study and manipulate lignin metabolism in plants in order to improve biomass properties for biotechnological applications.

“Human Genomics and Precision Medicine: Exploring Genetic Variability and Inborn Errors of Immunity”

Speaker: Dra. Fernanda Sales
Professor at Federal University of Rio Grande do Sul. She serves as head of the Experimental Research Center at the Hospital de Clínicas de Porto Alegre, conducting research in Genomics and Personalized Medicine.

This lecture addressed the genetic variability of the Brazilian human genome, recognized as one of the most diverse in the world. The impacts of this diversity on human health were discussed, with emphasis on responses to infections, inborn errors of immunity, and the perspectives of precision medicine for the Brazilian population.

“Lymphocyte Expansion for Cancer Immunotherapy”

Speaker: Dra. Lúcia Silla
Professor at Federal University of Rio Grande do Sul and coordinator of the Cell Therapy Center of the National Cell Therapy Network at the Hospital de Clínicas de Porto Alegre. Her main area of expertise focuses on cell therapy and bone marrow transplantation, particularly the use of NK (Natural Killer) cells.

The lecture explored lymphocyte expansion for Cancer Immunotherapy from the perspective of a researcher and clinician working directly with patients in the hospital environment.

DAY 2 (12/11/2025)

“Mycogenic Biomolecules: Promising Functional Agents for Biotechnology and Nanotechnology”

Speaker: Dra. Ana Olívia de Souza
Professor at Federal University of São Paulo, working in the areas of Bioremediation, Biofuels, Environmental Microbiology, and Biotechnological Processes.

The lecture addressed the potential of fungal-derived (mycogenic) biomolecules as functional agents in biotechnology and nanotechnology. The focus was on their unique properties related to green synthesis, biodegradability, and low toxicity, as well as their innovative applications in bioremediation, medicine, and sustainable agriculture.

“Biofabrication of Micro Bone Tissue”

Speaker: Dra. Katiucia Batista
Professor at University of São Paulo, working in the field of Bone Bioengineering. She leads the Laboratory of Extracellular Matrix Biology and Cell Interaction (LabMec), focusing on 3D cell models, development of novel biomaterials and bioinks for 3D bioprinting, and new biomedical devices based on microfluidics.

This lecture presented modern approaches for the biofabrication of micro bone tissue using mixed spheroids composed of dental pulp stem cells (DPSCs), endothelial cells, macrophages, and hybrid hydrogels containing nanohydroxyapatite.

“From Traditional Breeding to Genome Editing: New Approaches in Non-Saccharomyces Yeasts for Enology”

Speaker: Dra. Ana Paula Delamare
Professor at University of Caxias do Sul, working in the fields of Fermentation and Bioprocesses.

This lecture explored the role of non-Saccharomyces yeasts in enology, highlighting their historical use, their reintroduction into the wine industry, and their importance for increasing aromatic complexity and fermentation control. It also discussed traditional strain improvement methods and modern genome editing tools, as well as their impacts on the quality, diversity, and sustainability of wine production.

Entrepreneurship Session 1

Agronômica
This lecture introduced Agronômica, a laboratory specialized in phytosanitary diagnostics and consulting headquartered in Porto Alegre.

Promega
This lecture introduced Promega, a global biotechnology leader at the forefront of scientific innovation.

“The Use of Environmental Biotechnology as a Tool for Sustainable Development”

Speaker: Dra. Elen Aquino
Professor at Butantan Institute, working in Biotechnology and Nanotechnology of fungi isolated from mangroves and the Caatinga biome.

This lecture discussed the use of environmental biotechnology as a tool for sustainable development, highlighting applications such as wastewater treatment, bioremediation, bioenergy production, and waste valorization. It also emphasized the role of biotechnological processes in reducing environmental impacts, promoting the efficient use of natural resources, and supporting the transition toward a circular bioeconomy.

“Caseous Lymphadenitis: New Strategies for an Effective Vaccine”

Speaker: Dra. Sibele Borsuk
Professor at Federal University of Pelotas, working in Recombinant Vaccines and Molecular Biology.

This lecture discussed the development of recombinant vaccines as innovative strategies for controlling infectious diseases in both humans and animals. It highlighted research on DNA, subunit, and vectored vaccines for caseous lymphadenitis, presenting promising results obtained over the past 15 years.

“Recombinant BCG: New Immunotherapy Strategies Against Bladder Cancer and Melanoma”

Speaker: Fabiana Seixas
Professor at Federal University of Pelotas, working in the field of Recombinant Vaccines.

This lecture discussed advances in the development of recombinant BCG designed to stimulate targeted immune responses. Mycobacterium bovis BCG is globally recognized as a versatile vaccine vector with potential applications in several diseases, including cancer. The focus was on new immunotherapy strategies against bladder cancer and melanoma, addressing experimental results, translational perspectives, and future challenges associated with this innovative approach.

DAY 3 (13/11/2025)

“Gene Manipulation Strategies for Functional Studies in Pathogenic Yeasts”

Speaker: Dra. Lívia Kmetzsch
Professor at Federal University of Rio Grande do Sul, working in the field of Microbiology and Human Health, specifically with fungi of the Cryptococcus genus.

The lecture addressed gene manipulation strategies applied to the study of human pathogenic yeasts, focusing on Cryptococcus neoformans and C. gattii. Examples of gene deletion and overexpression were presented, highlighting correlations with phenotypes and the use of different promoters for functional analyses and for understanding virulence mechanisms.

“Integration of Computational Tools in the Analysis of Immunological Interactions”

Speaker: Dr. Maurício Menegatti Rigo
Assistant Professor at Federal University of Rio Grande do Sul. Has experince in the field of Immunogenetics, with emphasis on Bioinformatics for vaccine development, as well as in the areas of Computational Biology and Epigenetics.

This lecture presented an overview of computational tools applied to immunology, highlighting their utility in the analysis and prediction of interactions such as antigen-antibody and peptide-MHC-TCR interactions. The discussion also explored how these resources can drive the discovery of vaccines, therapeutic antibodies, and biomarkers.

“Regulation of Microalgae Metabolism via Nanosystems for Lipid Production”

Speaker: Dra. Flavia Vischi Winck
Professor at University of São Paulo, working in Biotechnology involving algae and microalgae.

This lecture discussed the potential of microalgae for sustainable biofuel production and highlighted how integrative omics approaches can help understand the regulation of lipid synthesis. It also explored the use of nanoparticle-mediated gene silencing to induce lipid accumulation in microalgae, demonstrating the potential of innovative technologies for microalgal biotechnology.

Entrepreneurship Session 2

Biosens
This lecture explored the development pathway of sensors for laboratory diagnostics, discussing technological innovation and applications in biotechnology.

Bioaddera
This lecture presented the company, its products, and some of the research results achieved so far.

Ecosynth
This lecture addressed the role of Biotechnology in transforming industry, discussing how academic knowledge can be transferred to traditional industrial sectors to generate positive socio-environmental impact.

“Bioinformatics in De-Extinction: Digital Maps for Biological Time Travel”

Speaker: Dr. Rodrigo Ligabue Braun
Professor at Federal University of Health Sciences of Porto Alegre. He has experience in the field of Bioinformatics, mainly working on molecular modeling, docking, molecular dynamics, and molecular evolution.

This lecture explored the role of Bioinformatics and Synthetic Biology in de-extinction approaches, highlighting how computational tools can identify key genes, reconstruct genomic information, and support the development of functional organisms capable of contributing to ecosystem restoration and biodiversity conservation.

“Bioprocesses and Bioreactors: A General Approach”

Speaker: Dr. Marco Ayub
Professor at Federal University of Rio Grande do Sul, working in the areas of Bioprocesses and Optimization of Fermentative Processes and Bioreactors.

This lecture provided an overview of bioprocess technology and the role of bioreactors in scaling biotechnological processes from laboratory research to industrial production. It also discussed general aspects related to the design, operation, and application of bioreactors in biotechnology.

“One Ring to Protect Them All, and with Iron Biofortify Them: The Role of Vacuolar Transporters in Iron Movement in Plants”

Speaker: Dr. Felipe Klein Ricachenevsky
Professor at Federal University of Rio Grande do Sul, working in Plant Genetic Engineering and Plant Physiology, with studies focused on rice biofortification with iron.

This lecture demonstrated how plants edited for two vacuolar iron transporters are being used both to increase iron concentration in rice seeds and to better understand the role of these proteins in iron movement within plants.
